# ICH E3 Clinical Study Report Structure

Use this chapter when reviewing a Clinical Study Report (CSR) or a CSR-derived manuscript to confirm the document follows the ICH E3 ("Structure and Content of Clinical Study Reports") section structure and that each section contains what regulators expect. Check that sections exist, are in the right place, and are internally consistent with each other, the protocol, and the registry entry. Flag missing, misplaced, or contradictory content.

The ICH E3 report is a single integrated report of one study (efficacy + safety). It has 16 numbered top-level sections plus a synopsis and 16-section appendices block. Below, each section lists what must appear and the concrete things a reviewer checks.

## How to use this chapter
- For each numbered section, confirm presence first, then adequacy.
- Cross-check every claim in the synopsis, results, and discussion against the methods, the protocol, the statistical analysis plan (SAP), and the registry record.
- Anchor every finding to a specific section number and page/heading so the author can locate it.
- Severity convention used here: **Major** = affects interpretability, regulatory acceptability, or could mislead on benefit/risk; **Minor** = completeness/clarity/formatting that does not change conclusions.

## Synopsis
A standalone short summary (E3 recommends roughly 3 pages) that mirrors the full report. A reader should be able to understand the study from the synopsis alone.
- [ ] Brief description of objectives, design, methods, subject population, treatments, and duration.
- [ ] Numeric results for the primary (and key secondary) endpoints with the same effect estimates and CIs as the body.
- [ ] Safety summary (deaths, SAEs, discontinuations due to AEs).
- [ ] Conclusion consistent with the body Discussion.
- **Decision rule:** any number, endpoint, denominator, or conclusion in the synopsis that does not match the body is a finding. Direction of effect and significance must match exactly.

## 1. Title page
- [ ] Study title, protocol/study identifier, study phase, investigational product, indication.
- [ ] Sponsor name, dates (study initiation, completion/early termination), date of the report and version.
- [ ] Sponsor responsible person / signatory and GCP compliance statement reference.
- **Flag:** no statement of GCP compliance anywhere in the report (E3 expects one).

## 2. Synopsis
(See the standalone Synopsis section above — E3 numbers it here.)

## 3. Table of contents
- [ ] Complete TOC for the report including location of tables, figures, and appendices.

## 4. List of abbreviations and definitions of terms
- [ ] All abbreviations and study-specific terms defined.

## 5. Ethics
- [ ] IRB/IEC review and approval statement (and that approval covered the protocol and amendments).
- [ ] Statement that the study was conducted per the Declaration of Helsinki / GCP.
- [ ] How informed consent was obtained (and re-consent after amendments where relevant).
- **Flag (Major):** consent or ethics-approval statement absent in a study involving human subjects.

## 6. Investigators and study administrative structure
- [ ] Principal investigators / sites, and the administrative structure (CRO, central labs, steering/data monitoring committees, central readers).
- [ ] Reference to the appendix listing investigators and their qualifications/signatures.

## 7. Introduction
- [ ] Brief rationale and context: nature/significance of the indication, prior data, and what this study contributes.
- [ ] Reference to development plan/guidance the study addresses where relevant.

## 8. Study objectives
- [ ] Statement of overall objectives and specific hypotheses, separated into primary and secondary.
- **Decision rule:** objectives here must map one-to-one to endpoints in Section 9.5 and analyses in 9.7. A primary objective with no corresponding pre-specified primary endpoint and analysis is a finding.

## 9. Investigational plan
The methods core of the CSR. Subsections:

### 9.1 Overall study design and plan
- [ ] Design type (parallel, crossover, factorial), control type, blinding, allocation ratio, and a design schematic/flow diagram.
- [ ] Duration of treatment and follow-up periods.

### 9.2 Discussion of study design, including choice of control groups
- [ ] Justification of the design and the choice of control (placebo, active, dose-response, historical). For non-inferiority/equivalence, the rationale for the margin.

### 9.3 Selection of study population
- [ ] 9.3.1 Inclusion criteria; 9.3.2 Exclusion criteria; 9.3.3 Removal of subjects from therapy or assessment (withdrawal criteria and how withdrawn subjects were handled).

### 9.4 Treatments
- [ ] Treatments administered, dose/regimen, IP identity (batch/lot), method of assigning subjects to treatment, dose selection rationale, blinding details, and prior/concomitant therapy rules.
- [ ] Treatment compliance measurement.

### 9.5 Efficacy and safety variables
- [ ] 9.5.1 Efficacy and safety measurements assessed and flowchart/schedule of assessments.
- [ ] 9.5.2 Appropriateness of measurements (validated instruments, established endpoints).
- [ ] 9.5.3 Primary efficacy variable explicitly identified.
- **Decision rule:** there must be exactly one clearly designated primary endpoint (or a clearly defined co-primary set with stated multiplicity handling). Ambiguity here is a Major finding.

### 9.6 Data quality assurance
- [ ] QA/QC methods: monitoring, data management, central labs/readers, training.

### 9.7 Statistical methods planned in the protocol and determination of sample size
- [ ] 9.7.1 Statistical and analytical plans: analysis populations (ITT, per-protocol, safety), handling of missing data, multiplicity, interim analyses and stopping rules, covariates/adjustment.
- [ ] 9.7.2 Determination of sample size: stated assumptions (effect size, variability, power, alpha) and the resulting N.
- **Decision rule:** sample size assumptions must be pre-specified; post-hoc justification fitted to the observed result is a finding.

### 9.8 Changes in the conduct of the study or planned analyses
- [ ] All protocol amendments and all changes to the planned analyses, with dates and whether they occurred before or after unblinding/database lock.
- **Flag (Major):** an analysis change (e.g., redefining the primary endpoint or analysis population) made after unblinding without explicit disclosure of timing and rationale.

## 10. Study subjects
### 10.1 Disposition of subjects
- [ ] A subject disposition flow (screened, randomized/enrolled, treated, completed, discontinued) with reasons for discontinuation by group. This is the CSR analogue of the CONSORT flow diagram.
### 10.2 Protocol deviations
- [ ] Important protocol deviations described and, where relevant, tabulated by category and group, with their handling in the analyses.

## 11. Efficacy evaluation
### 11.1 Data sets analysed
- [ ] Definition of each analysis population and exactly which subjects were in each, with denominators per group.
### 11.2 Demographic and other baseline characteristics
- [ ] Baseline demographics and disease characteristics by group; comment on between-group comparability.
### 11.3 Measurements of treatment compliance
- [ ] Compliance/exposure summarized by group.
### 11.4 Efficacy results and tabulations of individual subject data
- [ ] 11.4.1 Analysis of efficacy: primary endpoint result with effect estimate and 95% CI (not p-value alone), then secondary endpoints.
- [ ] 11.4.2 Statistical/analytical issues actually encountered (handling of dropouts, missing data, covariate adjustment, multicenter/pooling).
- [ ] 11.4.3 Tabulation of individual response data (or reference to appendix).
- [ ] 11.4.4 Drug dose, concentration, and relationship to response where applicable.
- [ ] 11.4.5 Drug-drug and drug-disease interactions.
- [ ] 11.4.6 By-subject displays where relevant.
- [ ] 11.4.7 Efficacy conclusions.
- **Decision rule:** the analysis population used for the primary efficacy analysis must match what was pre-specified (typically ITT/full analysis set for superiority). A switch to per-protocol as the primary basis without justification is a finding.

## 12. Safety evaluation
### 12.1 Extent of exposure
- [ ] Duration, dose, and number of subjects exposed (person-time where relevant).
### 12.2 Adverse events
- [ ] 12.2.1 Brief summary of AEs.
- [ ] 12.2.2 Display of AEs (by MedDRA SOC/PT, severity, and relationship), by group with denominators.
- [ ] 12.2.3 Analysis of AEs.
- [ ] 12.2.4 Listing of AEs by subject (or appendix reference).
### 12.3 Deaths, other serious adverse events, and other significant adverse events
- [ ] 12.3.1 Listings of deaths, SAEs, and discontinuations due to AEs.
- [ ] 12.3.2 Narratives for each death, each SAE, and certain other significant AEs.
- [ ] 12.3.3 Analysis and discussion of deaths/SAEs.
### 12.4 Clinical laboratory evaluation
- [ ] Lab listings, changes over time, and individual clinically significant abnormalities.
### 12.5 Vital signs, physical findings, and other observations related to safety.
### 12.6 Safety conclusions.
- **Decision rule:** every death and SAE must be both tabulated and narrated. A death listed in a table with no narrative is a Major finding.

## 13. Discussion and overall conclusions
- [ ] Integrated benefit-risk discussion grounded in the study's own efficacy and safety results.
- [ ] Discussion of limitations, clinical relevance, and consistency with prior evidence.
- **Decision rule:** conclusions must not exceed what the data support (e.g., claiming efficacy from a non-significant primary endpoint, or generalizing beyond the studied population). Overreach is a Major finding.

## 14. Tables, figures, and graphs referred to but not included in the text
- [ ] In-text reference tables/figures present and consistent with the body text numbers.

## 15. Reference list
- [ ] Complete references for methods, instruments, and prior data cited.

## 16. Appendices
E3 specifies a structured appendix block. Check for presence (or an explicit statement of location/availability):
- [ ] 16.1 Study information: protocol and amendments; sample CRF; IEC/IRB and consent documentation; investigator list and signatures; randomization scheme/codes; audit certificates; the statistical methods documentation (SAP); inter-laboratory standardization; publications from the study.
- [ ] 16.2 Subject data listings: discontinued subjects, protocol deviations, subjects excluded from analysis, demographic data, compliance/exposure, individual efficacy response data, AE listings, and listings of individual lab measurements.
- [ ] 16.3 Case report forms (deaths, SAEs, withdrawals for AEs).
- [ ] 16.4 Individual subject data listings (if required).
- **Flag (Minor→Major):** if the SAP/statistical methods documentation or the protocol-with-amendments is neither included nor referenced as an available appendix.

## Cross-cutting consistency checks
- [ ] Synopsis numbers == body numbers == in-text table/figure numbers.
- [ ] Objectives (8) ↔ endpoints (9.5) ↔ planned analyses (9.7) ↔ reported results (11/12) form a complete, traceable chain.
- [ ] Denominators are consistent across disposition (10.1), data sets analysed (11.1), baseline (11.2), and each results table.
- [ ] Protocol amendments (9.8) explain any methods text that differs from the original protocol.
- [ ] Registry entry (primary endpoint, population, key secondary endpoints) matches the CSR.

## Red flags to flag
Raise these explicitly, anchored to section and page:
- **Primary endpoint switch:** the primary endpoint in results (11.4.1) differs from the registered/pre-specified endpoint (registry, protocol, SAP) without disclosure in 9.8. *(Major)*
- **Analysis-population mismatch:** "ITT" (or full analysis set) is claimed but per-protocol denominators are used, or randomized subjects are silently dropped from the primary analysis. *(Major)*
- **Denominator drift:** N per group differs between disposition, baseline, and efficacy tables with no reconciliation. *(Major)*
- **Post-unblinding analysis changes:** changes to the SAP or endpoint definitions introduced after unblinding/database lock, presented as pre-specified. *(Major)*
- **Sample size reverse-engineered:** power/effect-size assumptions in 9.7.2 appear chosen to match the observed result rather than pre-specified. *(Major)*
- **Unjustified non-inferiority margin:** NI/equivalence margin not pre-specified or not clinically justified in 9.2. *(Major)*
- **Selective/relabeled endpoints:** a pre-specified secondary or exploratory endpoint reported as if primary, or significant secondaries emphasized while a non-significant primary is downplayed. *(Major)*
- **Missing-data hand-waving:** dropouts/missing data not quantified or handled by a method not pre-specified (e.g., switching to a method that favors the result); no sensitivity analysis. *(Major)*
- **Effect without precision:** primary result reported as a p-value only, with no effect estimate and 95% CI. *(Major)*
- **Deaths/SAEs without narratives:** any death or SAE tabulated but not narrated in 12.3.2. *(Major)*
- **Conclusion overreach:** Discussion (13) or synopsis claims efficacy/safety beyond the data (non-significant primary spun as positive, generalization beyond studied population, benefit-risk asserted without integrating harms). *(Major)*
- **Multiplicity ignored:** multiple endpoints/timepoints/subgroups tested with no pre-specified multiplicity control, then significant findings highlighted. *(Major)*
- **Subgroup spin:** a post-hoc subgroup presented as a primary conclusion without pre-specification or interaction testing. *(Major)*
- **No GCP/ethics statement:** GCP compliance, IRB/IEC approval, or informed-consent statement missing. *(Major)*
- **Protocol/SAP not available:** protocol-with-amendments or statistical methods documentation neither appended nor referenced. *(Minor→Major depending on regulatory context)*
- **Synopsis ≠ body:** any numeric or directional discrepancy between synopsis and body. *(Minor if a typo; Major if it changes the conclusion)*
- **Important protocol deviations not described (10.2)** or not reflected in analysis decisions. *(Minor→Major)*
- **Missing assessment schedule/flowchart in 9.5.1.** *(Minor)*
- **Abbreviations/terms undefined (Section 4).** *(Minor)*

## Severity quick-reference
- **Major:** anything affecting which endpoint/population/analysis defines the result, undisclosed post-hoc changes, missing safety narratives for deaths/SAEs, conclusions unsupported by data, missing ethics/GCP/consent statements.
- **Minor:** formatting, missing abbreviations, incomplete TOC, isolated typographical inconsistencies that do not change interpretation, missing in-text figures that exist in appendices.
