# Clinical Study Report — Excerpt

**Study Title:** A Phase 3, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled, Parallel-Group Study to Evaluate the Efficacy and Safety of Dapagliravir 50 mg Once Daily in Adults with Moderate-to-Severe Plaque Psoriasis

**Protocol Number:** DPV-PSO-301
**Sponsor:** Northbridge Therapeutics, Inc.
**Phase:** 3
**Report Status:** Final, Version 1.0
**Report Date:** 14 March 2025

---

## Synopsis

This Phase 3 study evaluated the efficacy and safety of dapagliravir 50 mg once daily compared with placebo in adults with moderate-to-severe chronic plaque psoriasis over a 16-week double-blind treatment period. A total of 612 participants were randomized 1:1 to dapagliravir (n = 306) or placebo (n = 306). The primary efficacy endpoint was the proportion of participants achieving a 75% or greater reduction from baseline in the Psoriasis Area and Severity Index (PASI 75) at Week 16. The study met its primary objective: 64.1% of participants in the dapagliravir group achieved PASI 75 at Week 16 versus 9.8% in the placebo group (difference 54.3 percentage points; 95% CI 47.9 to 60.7; p < 0.001). Dapagliravir was generally well tolerated, with an adverse event profile consistent with the known safety of the class.

---

## 1. Objectives

### 1.1 Primary Objective

To compare the efficacy of dapagliravir 50 mg once daily with placebo, as measured by the proportion of participants achieving PASI 75 at Week 16.

### 1.2 Secondary Objectives

- To compare the proportion of participants achieving a Physician's Global Assessment (PGA) score of 0 (clear) or 1 (almost clear) at Week 16.
- To compare the proportion of participants achieving PASI 90 at Week 16.
- To evaluate the safety and tolerability of dapagliravir over 16 weeks.

---

## 2. Investigational Plan

### 2.1 Overall Study Design

This was a multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group study conducted at 48 sites across North America and Europe. Eligible participants were randomized in a 1:1 ratio to receive either dapagliravir 50 mg or matching placebo, administered orally once daily for 16 weeks. Randomization was stratified by baseline PASI severity (moderate vs. severe) and prior biologic exposure (yes vs. no).

### 2.2 Study Population

Adults aged 18 to 75 years with a diagnosis of chronic plaque psoriasis for at least 6 months, a baseline PASI score of at least 12, a PGA score of at least 3, and body surface area involvement of at least 10% were eligible. Key exclusion criteria included guttate, erythrodermic, or pustular psoriasis; active infection; and use of systemic psoriasis therapy within 4 weeks of baseline.

### 2.3 Endpoints

The primary efficacy endpoint was the proportion of participants achieving a reduction of at least 75% from baseline in the PASI score (PASI 75) at Week 16. Participants who discontinued study treatment for any reason prior to Week 16 and who had no Week 16 assessment were considered non-responders for the primary endpoint analysis.

---

## 3. Statistical Methods

### 3.1 Analysis Populations

The **Full Analysis Set (FAS)** comprised all randomized participants, analyzed according to the treatment to which they were randomized, consistent with the intention-to-treat (ITT) principle. The FAS was the primary population for all efficacy analyses. The **Per-Protocol Set (PPS)** comprised all FAS participants who completed the 16-week treatment period without a major protocol deviation. The **Safety Set** comprised all participants who received at least one dose of study drug.

### 3.2 Primary Efficacy Analysis

The primary endpoint, the proportion of participants achieving PASI 75 at Week 16, was analyzed using a Cochran-Mantel-Haenszel (CMH) test stratified by the randomization strata. The difference in response rates between treatment groups, with a 95% confidence interval, was estimated. The primary analysis was performed on the Full Analysis Set. Participants with missing Week 16 PASI assessments were imputed as non-responders (non-responder imputation, NRI).

### 3.3 Handling of Missing Data

For continuous secondary endpoints, missing values at Week 16 were imputed using last observation carried forward (LOCF). This approach was considered appropriate given the relatively short duration of the treatment period and the expectation that most discontinuations would occur late in the study.

### 3.4 Sample Size Justification

A sample size of 300 participants per group was selected to provide at least 90% power to detect a difference in PASI 75 response rates of 25 percentage points (assuming 40% in the dapagliravir group and 15% in the placebo group), using a two-sided test at the 0.05 significance level, allowing for a 10% dropout rate.

### 3.5 Multiplicity

The primary endpoint was tested at the two-sided 0.05 significance level. Secondary endpoints were tested using a fixed-sequence hierarchical testing procedure to control the family-wise type I error rate at 0.05.

## 4. Results

### 4.1 Participant Disposition

A total of 612 participants were randomized (306 dapagliravir, 306 placebo). Of these, 571 (93.3%) completed the 16-week treatment period. Discontinuations were balanced across groups: 19 (6.2%) in the dapagliravir group and 22 (7.2%) in the placebo group. The most common reasons for discontinuation were withdrawal of consent and loss to follow-up.

### 4.2 Baseline Characteristics

Demographic and baseline disease characteristics were well balanced between treatment groups. The mean age was 46.3 years (SD 13.1), 58% of participants were male, and the mean baseline PASI score was 19.8 (SD 6.4). Prior biologic exposure was reported in 31% of participants.

### 4.3 Primary Efficacy Endpoint

At Week 16, the proportion of participants achieving a reduction of at least 50% from baseline in the PASI score (PASI 75) was significantly greater in the dapagliravir group than in the placebo group. In the Per-Protocol Set, 64.1% of participants in the dapagliravir group achieved PASI 75 compared with 9.8% in the placebo group (difference 54.3 percentage points; 95% CI 47.9 to 60.7; p < 0.001).

The treatment effect was consistent across the prespecified subgroups of baseline severity and prior biologic exposure.

### 4.4 Secondary Efficacy Endpoints

At Week 16, 52.0% of participants in the dapagliravir group achieved a PGA score of 0 or 1, compared with 6.5% in the placebo group (p < 0.001). PASI 90 was achieved by 41.2% of dapagliravir participants versus 3.6% of placebo participants (p < 0.001). All secondary endpoints were statistically significant in the prespecified hierarchical testing order.

## 5. Safety

### 5.1 Extent of Exposure

All 612 randomized participants received at least one dose of study drug and were included in the Safety Set. The mean duration of exposure was 15.1 weeks in both groups.

### 5.2 Adverse Events

Treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs) were reported in 198 (64.7%) participants in the dapagliravir group and 171 (55.9%) participants in the placebo group. The most frequently reported TEAEs in the dapagliravir group were nasopharyngitis, headache, and upper respiratory tract infection. Most TEAEs were mild or moderate in severity.

Serious adverse events were uncommon. Two serious adverse events were reported in the dapagliravir group (one appendicitis, one cholecystitis), both considered unrelated to study drug. No deaths occurred during the study.

### 5.3 Discontinuations Due to Adverse Events

Adverse events leading to discontinuation occurred in 7 (2.3%) participants in the dapagliravir group and 5 (1.6%) participants in the placebo group.

### 5.4 Safety Summary

Overall, dapagliravir 50 mg once daily was well tolerated. Serious infections were rare and occurred at a low frequency comparable to placebo. No new safety signals were identified during the 16-week treatment period.

---

## 6. Discussion and Conclusions

In this Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study, dapagliravir 50 mg once daily demonstrated superior efficacy compared with placebo for the treatment of moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis. The primary endpoint was met, with a clinically meaningful and statistically significant difference in PASI 75 response at Week 16. Secondary endpoints supported the primary finding.

The safety profile observed in this study was consistent with the known profile of the class, and dapagliravir was generally well tolerated over the 16-week treatment period. These results support the use of dapagliravir as an effective treatment option for adults with moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis.
